# Go-To-Market Strategy

## The Problem
Startups are accumulating SaaS subscriptions faster than ever, driven heavily by the rapid proliferation of generative AI tools. Founders and fractional CFOs lack visibility into API usage inefficiency, duplicate tooling (e.g., paying for GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and ChatGPT Enterprise simultaneously), and over-provisioned seats.

## The Wedge
TokenLeak acts as a "Trojan Horse" for financial intelligence. 

Instead of selling a complex, heavy accounting integration, TokenLeak offers an instant, self-serve audit. 
Founders input their stack in 60 seconds and receive an executive-ready report detailing exactly how much runway they can recover.

## Target Audience
- **Primary:** Technical Founders and CEOs of Seed to Series B startups.
- **Secondary:** Fractional CFOs managing burn rates for multiple portfolio companies.

## Acquisition Channels
1. **Engineering-Led Growth**: "Show off your Stack Complexity Risk". Generating shareable, visually impressive "Leak Radar" charts that founders naturally want to post on Twitter/X or LinkedIn.
2. **VC Partnerships**: Distributing the tool via Y Combinator, Techstars, or Andreessen Horowitz portfolio networks as a standard "burn audit" checklist item.
3. **SEO & Content**: Benchmarking reports (e.g., "The average Series A startup wastes $12,000/yr on idle AI seats").
